Understanding Intercultural Transitions

Migrant-host relationship: Assimilation

Example: A family with a newborn baby moves from Japan to the United States. They experience many cultural differences at their new home, not limited to but including language. The family decides to only teach their child English, in hopes of them fitting in better. Time goes by and the longer they live there, the more mainstream culture they adopt. This is an example of assimilation, which is giving up one's cultural identity to adopt that of the mainstream cultural identity.
